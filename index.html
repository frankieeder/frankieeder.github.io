Moonset I

why; are you paying for this?

(three-channel video installation)

cw: sexual violence, flashing lights
360 Degree Installation View
Isolating and algorithmically affecting scenes from cinema that romanticize sexual manipulation, "why; are you paying for this?" offers alternate, synchronized views that question physical and emotional autonomy of survivors in the digital age of Hollywood.

Channel 1 (left): Unmodified, theatrical excerpts
Channel 2 (middle): Heavily modified excerpt that cycles at a high human heart rate.
Channel 3 (right): Degraded view of theatrical excerpts, physically opposing Channel 1.

Concatenations II

(photo collection)

Created by algorithmically combining, filtering, and disfiguring sequenced images of LGBTQ+ individuals, Concatenations II investigates and deconstructs ideas of queer visual representation, recognition, censorship, and identity in a modern-day technology-driven socioeconomic landscape.

Cinematic Colorization

Analyzes the spatial and temporal characteristics of color in cinema using a newly collected dataset of 1800+ trailers. Utilizes knowledge gained to explore color-based genre classification and automatic color grading. Completed collaboratively with Cecilia Zhang (PhD, UC Berkeley) and Vivien Nguyen (MS, UC Berkeley).

Amodal3DetTF

A TensorFlow re-implementation of object detection algorithms presented in "Amodal Detection of 3D Objects: Inferring 3D Bounding Boxes from 2D Ones in RGB-Depth Images" (Deng et al.) for use on mobile devices. Completed as a research assistant in UC Berkeley's Video and Image Processing Lab

Orthogonal Structure Detection

Extends use cases of a CVPR 2016 paper, proving comparable results on original dataset and invariance to wall shape with an IOU decrease of less than 5% on non-planar wall cases. No learning required.
